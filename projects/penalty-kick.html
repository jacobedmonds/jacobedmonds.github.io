Overview

Team-based analysis investigating whether a kicker’s run-up step count is associated with penalty kick success or shot placement. Built a custom dataset by combining public match data with video-based coding of each penalty event.

Key Idea: Step Routine Categories

Step routines are defined using the empirical distribution of steps:

  • Shortened: fewer than 4 steps
  • Normal: 4–8 steps (25th–75th percentile range)
  • Lengthened: greater than 8 steps

Note: In the ball-placement heatmaps, zone percentages are computed relative to all attempts within each routine, including off-target attempts (pos=7). Off-target rates are reported in captions.

Key Findings

  • Run-up step count was not statistically associated with scoring success in our sample
  • Step routine categories did not show meaningful differences in shot placement patterns
  • Practical implication: run-up length appears more stylistic than a reliable performance lever

Methods

  • Cleaning + standardization using tidyverse
  • Spatial heatmaps using goal-zone mapping (1–6 in-goal, 7 off-target)
  • Shapiro–Wilk normality checks; Wilcoxon rank-sum for step differences
