Billionaires EDA: Education & Industry

Team-based Exploratory data analysis using global billionaire data to study industry concentration and median net worth across education enrollment levels.

Project Overview

This project analyzes how billionaire industry representation and median net worth differ between countries with high and low gross primary enrollment rates. Countries were split into high and low enrollment groups using the global median enrollment rate.

Research Questions

  • Do billionaire industries differ between countries with high vs low primary enrollment?
  • Does median net worth vary by industry across enrollment groups?

Data & Methodology

  • Global billionaire dataset with industry classification and net worth
  • Country-level gross primary enrollment rates used as education proxy
  • Exploratory comparisons using grouped summaries and faceted visualizations

Key Findings

  • Countries with higher primary enrollment rates show more diversified billionaire industries, particularly in manufacturing, healthcare, and fashion & retail.
  • Countries with lower enrollment rates tend to have higher median net worth in several industries, including finance, logistics, and gambling.
  • The relationship between education and wealth accumulation varies substantially by industry.
